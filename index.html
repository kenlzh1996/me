About Me

Born and raised in Zhuhai, China, I completed my BS in Food Science and Technology at University of California, Davis and MS in Biochemical and Molecular Nutrition at Tufts University. I am currently a PhD student in the Division of Nutritional Sciences at Cornell University. My research focuses on developing advanced tools for protein subcellular localization prediction by integrating omics data and machine learning. I am also interested in bridging proteomics and mass spectrometry with artificial intelligence to address biological questions.

When I am not by my keyboard, I enjoy playing another keyboard (piano), hiking, and cooking. I am known for going on spontaneous road trips to explore new places or visit friends. Spot me at my favorite bar in town, you will see me sipping and chatting with friends.

Although I do not post regularly, but feel free to follow me on Twitter and LinkedIn.
